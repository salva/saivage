# F01 - RAG subsystem design addendum review

## Findings by axis

1. Facade-vs-primitives boundary policy - PASS. The addendum makes both primitive seams and the facade first-class entry points, explicitly rejects privileged/internal-only treatment, and lists concrete facade non-goals including no generic store, no caller schemas, no consumer shortcuts, no cross-dataset query, and no smart routing. Evidence: [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L21-L48](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L21-L48).

2. Watcher specification - CHANGES REQUESTED. The addendum covers most requested watcher mechanics: opt-in `watch` config, chokidar ESM/MIT/runtime dependency, build/cache/editor-temp exclusions layered with secret exclusions, debounce, reconcile, inotify-limit behavior without silent polling fallback, operator-selected polling for bind mounts/NFS, and flood handling. Evidence: [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L96-L132](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L96-L132), [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L136-L159](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L136-L159), [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L165-L176](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L165-L176), and [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L220-L228](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L220-L228). Two blockers remain:

- The watcher depends on "registered source roots" for watch scope and `reconcile()`, but the approved public surface only has roots inside per-call `IngestInput`; the addendum does not define how roots become registered or persisted for startup reconcile and zero-argument `dataset.reconcile()`. Evidence: [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L123-L159](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L123-L159), [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/02-design-r2.md#L77-L86](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/02-design-r2.md#L77-L86), and [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/03-plan-r2.md#L125](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/03-plan-r2.md#L125).
- The lock-awareness rule says the watcher waits and does not fail when explicit ingest holds the `proper-lockfile` lock, but the addendum drops the batch after a 30 second lock wait. That can lose the only event that should make a changed file query-visible. Evidence: [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L145-L149](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L145-L149).

3. Public surface additions - PASS. The only new dataset methods are `watch()`, `unwatch()`, and `reconcile()`, and the addendum explicitly rejects watcher-status, flush, subscription, and query-side additions. Evidence: [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L184-L190](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L184-L190).

Evidence: [saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L230-L286](saivage/SPEC/2026-05/rag-subsystem-design/F01-rag-subsystem/04-addendum-r1.md#L230-L286).

9. No emojis - PASS. I found no emoji characters in the addendum.

## Required Changes

1. Define how watcher source roots are registered and persisted. The revised addendum must say whether roots/include/exclude patterns move into dataset config or into a persisted source-root registry derived from `IngestInput`, and it must update the relevant implementation-plan deltas so `watch()`, startup reconcile, and explicit `reconcile()` have deterministic roots.

2. Remove the lock-wait batch-drop behavior or replace it with a design that cannot lose the only watcher event while explicit ingest holds the lock. The required behavior is that the watcher waits/coalesces behind `proper-lockfile` contention and eventually ingests the final coalesced state, without surfacing failure or dropping the batch solely because the explicit ingest took longer than a timeout.

3. Remove the two literal "dance" references and use self-contained future-work wording instead.

VERDICT: CHANGES_REQUESTED
